# Project Context – Internal Phishing Simulation

This document explains the business background, motivation, scope, and success criteria of the internal phishing simulation initiative.

The purpose is to demonstrate not only technical execution, but also organisational reasoning.

---

# 1️⃣ Business Motivation

Phishing remains one of the most common entry vectors in enterprise security incidents.

Despite technical controls (email filtering, endpoint protection, MFA), user behaviour remains a critical risk factor.

The organisation initiated this phishing simulation to:

- Measure real-world employee susceptibility.
- Identify behavioural risk patterns.
- Improve targeted awareness training.
- Strengthen organisational security posture.

This was positioned as a **risk reduction and education initiative**, not a punitive exercise.

---

# 2️⃣ Strategic Objectives

The campaign aimed to:

- Establish a behavioural baseline (click rate / interaction rate).
- Segment risk by department or function.
- Measure improvement over time.
- Align awareness training with real data.

Long-term objective:

Shift from reactive awareness training to data-driven awareness strategy.

---

# 3️⃣ Target Audience & Scope

## In Scope
- Approved internal employee population.
- Controlled campaign wave.
- Authorised tracking metrics (open / click).

## Out of Scope
- External users.
- Real credential harvesting.
- Production system interference.
- Any unauthorised data collection.

Scope boundaries were defined before technical deployment.

---

# 4️⃣ Success Metrics

Success was not defined solely by click rate.

Primary indicators:

- Campaign delivery success rate.
- Validity of event logging.
- Risk segmentation clarity.
- Actionable reporting output.
- Executive alignment on follow-up actions.

Longitudinal measurement (future campaigns) is key to evaluating improvement.

---

# 5️⃣ Communication Strategy

To maintain organisational trust:

- Executive sponsorship secured before launch.
- Key stakeholders informed (IT, Email Admin, SOC).
- Post-campaign transparency ensured.
- Framing focused on education, not blame.

Security awareness must reinforce trust, not damage it.

---

# 6️⃣ Risk Framing

Before execution, risk was evaluated across:

- Infrastructure stability
- Email reputation impact
- Data privacy exposure
- Employee morale impact
- SOC false-positive escalation risk

Mitigations were defined in governance documentation.

---

# 7️⃣ Organisational Learning Goals

Beyond metrics, the campaign aimed to improve:

- Cross-team coordination.
- Security program maturity.
- Data-driven risk discussions.
- Executive visibility into human-factor risk.

This aligns security awareness with enterprise risk management practices.

---

# 8️⃣ Interview Highlight Areas

Security Operations:
- Behavioural baseline measurement.
- Metrics beyond click rate.

Infrastructure:
- Scope control and isolation boundaries.

Governance:
- Executive sponsorship and risk assessment.

Leadership:
- Framing awareness initiatives constructively.
